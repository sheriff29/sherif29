A brief history of my library experience

75% Complete (success)75% Academic
15% Complete (warning)25% Public

Working as field supervisor in the natural resource sector strongly influenced my decision to become a librarian. A business strategy to woo local natural resource producers saw me teaming up with an outreach library startup to implement a tool library project for local resource producer communities. Through the success of the project, I came to realize a key aspect of librarianship: facilitating learning, even in remote, resource-starved settings.

Since then, I have sought to grow as information professional by keeping conscious of my duty to improve access to literacy for all irrespective of knowledge or linquistic background or capability. One way I strive to do this is by constantly searching for and acquiring new knowledge (or improving on existing ones) in areas such as technology, teaching, and community service.

Overall, my personal mission is to develop strong knowledge and technology competencies to enable me provide innovative information services and solutions to patrons in both academic and public settings. For more on my recent experiences, feel free to check out myResume
